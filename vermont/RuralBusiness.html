Feed and Supply Marketing Strategy for Rural Area Business¶

By Mia Murphy¶

12/01/2023¶

For a marketing strategy focused on a small, rural feed and supply company in the Upper Valley area, specifically between White River Junction, Vermont, and West Lebanon, New Hampshire, understanding the local demographics is crucial. Here's a summary of the demographic information for these areas:

White River Junction, Vermont:¶

  • Population: Approximately 2,776 residents.
  • Median Age: 37.5 years, which is slightly lower than the state average of 42.7 years.
  • Income: The estimated median household income in 2021 was $60,906.
  • Racial Composition: Predominantly White (85.3%), with smaller percentages of other races including Black or African American (0.9%).

West Lebanon, New Hampshire:¶

  • Population: Around 4,261 people.
  • Population Growth Rate: The area is experiencing a growth rate of 3.93%.
  • Median Household Income: In 2021, it was $66,934.
  • Racial Composition: Majority White (83.2%), with notable Asian (10.88%) and Black (3.31%) populations.

Lebanon, New Hampshire (for broader context):¶

  • Population: 16,431 residents, showing a significant increase since 2020.
  • Median Age: 38.1 years, with a slightly higher age median for females (40.3 years).
  • Gender Distribution: Females constitute a slightly larger part of the population (53.44%).
  • Racial Composition: Predominantly White (87.88%), followed by Asian (5.15%) and Black or African American (2.35%).

Dog Ownership Statistics in Vermont and New Hampshire:¶

  • In Vermont, more than two-thirds of residents (70.8%) own at least one pet.
  • Vermont has a high rate of cat ownership at 49.5% and, while not in the top 10 states for dog ownership, still has a considerable dog ownership rate of 37.7%.
  • New Hampshire's overall pet ownership rate is 51.8%, with 23.7% of households owning dogs.

Marketing Strategy Implications:¶

  1. Target Audience:
    • Focus on adults in the age range of 37-40 years, as they form a significant part of the population.
    • Tailor marketing efforts to appeal to a predominantly White demographic, but also consider the notable Asian and Black communities in West Lebanon.
  2. Income Sensitivity:
    • Given the median income levels, marketing strategies should consider the affordability of products, offering a range of options to cater to diverse income groups.
  3. Community Engagement:
    • Plan events and promotions that resonate with the local community's interests and demographics.
    • Consider family-oriented marketing strategies, as the demographics suggest a significant portion of the population might be families.
  4. Social Media and Advertising:
    • Use social media platforms to target the adult population effectively, focusing on content that appeals to the 37-40 age range.
    • Develop advertising campaigns that reflect the local community's demographic makeup, ensuring inclusivity and relevance.
  5. Product Diversification:
    • Given the diverse needs of the community, consider expanding the range of products to cater to different preferences and needs.

Community Engagement, Brand Loyalty and Local Competition¶

  1. Target Market Analysis:
    • Age and Income Brackets: Tailor marketing messages to resonate with the primary age groups identified in the survey. Offer pricing and deals that align with the predominant income brackets.
    • Dog Owners: Since a significant portion of the respondents are dog owners, the marketing strategy should heavily focus on dog-related products, services, and information.
  2. Purchasing Location Preferences:
    • Analyze the data to understand why some customers prefer shopping at places like Home Depot or grocery stores. This insight can help in adjusting product offerings, pricing, and marketing tactics.
    • Enhance the in-store experience to compete effectively with these larger stores. Personalized customer service and unique product offerings can be a differentiator.
  3. Community Engagement Strategies:
    • Events Sponsorship: Plan and sponsor local events that resonate with the community, such as dog shows, pet adoption days, and local fairs. These events can enhance visibility and brand recognition.
    • Partnerships with Local Organizations: Collaborate with local shelters, dog clubs, or schools to create community-centric initiatives. This not only boosts visibility but also demonstrates commitment to community welfare.
  4. Social Media and Online Presence:
    • Given the high usage of social media for local business information, an active online presence is crucial. Regular posts, engaging content, and interactive campaigns can draw in the digital audience.
    • Utilize social media analytics to track engagement and tailor content to the preferences of the audience. Also, consider online sales or promotions exclusive to social media followers.
  5. Feedback and Continuous Improvement:
    • Implement a system for collecting ongoing customer feedback, both in-store and online. This feedback can guide future product offerings, services, and marketing strategies.
    • Regularly update the product line and services based on customer preferences and market trends, especially focusing on high-demand items like dog food.
  6. Local SEO and Online Marketing:
    • Invest in local SEO strategies to ensure that the company appears in search results for relevant queries in the Upper Valley region.
    • Use targeted online advertising to reach potential customers in the specific geographic areas of White River Junction, Vermont, and West Lebanon, New Hampshire.
  7. Educational Workshops and Demonstrations:
    • Host educational events on pet care, nutrition, and other relevant topics. This not only positions the company as an expert in the field but also increases customer engagement and loyalty.

import pandas as pd
import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
import seaborn as sns
import warnings
warnings.filterwarnings('ignore', category=FutureWarning)

# Sample data generation for the survey results
np.random.seed(0)
survey_data = pd.DataFrame({
    "Age": np.random.choice(["18-25", "26-35", "36-45", "46-55", "56+"], 200, p=[0.15, 0.25, 0.20, 0.20, 0.20]),
    "Income Bracket": np.random.choice(["<20K", "20-40K", "40-60K", "60-80K", "80K+"], 200, p=[0.10, 0.20, 0.30, 0.25, 0.15]),
    "Dog Owner": np.random.choice(["Yes", "No"], 200, p=[0.7, 0.3]),
    "Purchasing Location Preference": np.random.choice(["Feed and Supply Store", "Grocery Store", "Home Depot", "Online"], 200, p=[0.25, 0.25, 0.25, 0.25]),
    "Interest in Community Events": np.random.choice(["Very Interested", "Somewhat Interested", "Neutral", "Not Interested"], 200, p=[0.25, 0.30, 0.20, 0.25]),
    "Social Media Usage for Local Business Info": np.random.choice(["Very Often", "Sometimes", "Rarely", "Never"], 200, p=[0.25, 0.35, 0.25, 0.15])
})

# Visualizations
plt.figure(figsize=(15, 10))

# Age Distribution
plt.subplot(2, 3, 1)
sns.countplot(x="Age", data=survey_data, palette="viridis")  # Using a different palette
plt.title("Age Distribution of Survey Respondents")
plt.xticks(rotation=45)

# Income Bracket Distribution
plt.subplot(2, 3, 2)
sns.countplot(x="Income Bracket", data=survey_data, palette="coolwarm")  # Using a different palette
plt.title("Income Bracket Distribution")
plt.xticks(rotation=45)

# Dog Owner Percentage
plt.subplot(2, 3, 3)
dog_owner_counts = survey_data["Dog Owner"].value_counts()
plt.pie(dog_owner_counts, labels=dog_owner_counts.index, autopct='%1.1f%%', startangle=140, colors=["#66b3ff","#ff9999"])  # Specifying colors
plt.title("Percentage of Dog Owners")

# Purchasing Location Preference
plt.subplot(2, 3, 4)
sns.countplot(x="Purchasing Location Preference", data=survey_data, palette="plasma")  # Using a different palette
plt.title("Purchasing Location Preference")
plt.xticks(rotation=45)

# Interest in Community Events
plt.subplot(2, 3, 5)
sns.countplot(x="Interest in Community Events", data=survey_data, palette="cubehelix")  # Using a different palette
plt.title("Interest in Community Events")
plt.xticks(rotation=45)

plt.subplot(2, 3, 6)
sns.countplot(x="Social Media Usage for Local Business Info", data=survey_data, palette="spring")  # Using a different palette
